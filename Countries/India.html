Taj Mahal (Agra, Uttar Pradesh): One of the most iconic symbols of India, the Taj Mahal is a white marble mausoleum built by Emperor Shah Jahan in memory of his beloved wife Mumtaz Mahal. It is renowned for its stunning architecture, intricate carvings, and beautiful gardens.
Jaipur, Rajasthan (Pink City): Jaipur is the capital of the Indian state of Rajasthan and is known for its vibrant culture and historical landmarks. The City Palace, Hawa Mahal (Palace of Winds), and Amer Fort are some of the must-visit attractions. The city is also famous for its colorful markets and traditional Rajasthani cuisine.
Varanasi, Uttar Pradesh: Varanasi, also known as Kashi, is one of the oldest continuously inhabited cities in the world and is a major spiritual hub for Hindus. The ghats along the Ganges River, Kashi Vishwanath Temple, and Sarnath (where Buddha gave his first sermon) are significant places to visit in Varanasi.
Kerala Backwaters (Alappuzha, Kerala): The backwaters of Kerala offer a unique and tranquil experience. Houseboat rides through the interconnected rivers, lakes, and canals provide a glimpse into the peaceful rural life of Kerala. The lush green landscapes, coconut palms, and traditional villages add to the charm of the backwaters.
Khajuraho Temples (Madhya Pradesh): The Khajuraho Group of Monuments is a UNESCO World Heritage Site known for its stunning medieval temples adorned with intricate and erotic sculptures. The temples were built by the Chandela dynasty between 950 and 1050 CE and are a remarkable example of medieval Indian architecture.
