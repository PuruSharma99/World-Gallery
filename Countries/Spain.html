La Sagrada Familia (Barcelona): One of the most iconic landmarks in Spain, La Sagrada Familia is a basilica designed by the renowned architect Antoni Gaudí. The unique and intricate architecture, both inside and out, makes it a must-visit destination in Barcelona.
Alhambra (Granada): The Alhambra is a stunning palace and fortress complex that showcases the pinnacle of Moorish art and architecture. Visitors can explore the intricate tilework, beautiful gardens, and historic structures that make up this UNESCO World Heritage site.
Prado Museum (Madrid): Located in the capital city, the Prado Museum is one of the most important art museums in the world. It houses an extensive collection of European art, with works by renowned artists such as Velázquez, Goya, and El Greco.
Park Güell (Barcelona): Another masterpiece by Antoni Gaudí, Park Güell is a public park that features colorful mosaic sculptures, unique architecture, and panoramic views of the city. It's a UNESCO World Heritage site and a popular attraction for both locals and tourists.
Seville Cathedral: The Seville Cathedral is the largest Gothic cathedral in the world and is known for its impressive architecture and historical significance.
Alcázar of Seville (Seville): Alcázar of Seville, a royal palace complex with stunning gardens, intricate tilework, and a mix of architectural styles.
