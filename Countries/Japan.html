Kyoto: Kyoto is renowned for its well-preserved classical temples, as well as gardens, traditional wooden houses, and geisha district (Gion). Visit the Fushimi Inari Shrine with its thousands of red torii gates, Kinkaku-ji (the Golden Pavilion), and the beautiful Arashiyama Bamboo Grove.
Tokyo: Japan's bustling capital, Tokyo, offers a mix of modern skyscrapers, historic temples, and vibrant street life. Don't miss the iconic Tokyo Tower, the historic Asakusa Senso-ji Temple, the trendy district of Shibuya with the famous Shibuya Crossing, and the electronic town Akihabara.
Hiroshima and Miyajima: Hiroshima is known for its tragic history as the first city to experience a nuclear attack during World War II. The Peace Memorial Park and Museum provide a poignant reminder of this history. A short ferry ride from Hiroshima takes you to Miyajima, famous for its "floating" torii gate at Itsukushima Shrine.
Nara: Nara is home to some of Japan's oldest and most significant temples. The Todai-ji Temple houses a giant Buddha statue, and Nara Park is known for its friendly deer that roam freely. The historic ambiance and cultural richness make Nara a must-visit destination.
Mount Fuji: Mount Fuji, Japan's highest peak, is an iconic symbol of the country. The surrounding Fuji Five Lakes region provides stunning views of the mountain. The Chureito Pagoda and the Hakone hot springs are popular spots to appreciate the beauty of Mount Fuji.
