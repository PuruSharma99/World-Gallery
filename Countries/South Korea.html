Gyeongbokgung Palace (Seoul): Gyeongbokgung, located in the heart of Seoul, is the largest of the Five Grand Palaces built during the Joseon Dynasty. Visitors can explore its beautiful architecture, traditional gardens, and witness the changing of the guard ceremony. The palace also houses the National Palace Museum of Korea, which displays various artifacts from Korean history.
Jeju Island: Jeju is a picturesque volcanic island known for its stunning natural beauty. Visitors can explore Hallasan, South Korea's highest mountain, visit the Manjanggul Cave, and relax on scenic beaches like Jungmun Saekdal Beach. The island's unique landscapes, waterfalls, and lava tubes make it a popular destination for nature lovers.
Bukchon Hanok Village (Seoul): Bukchon Hanok Village is a well-preserved traditional Korean village in the heart of Seoul. It features narrow alleys lined with hanok (traditional Korean houses), offering a glimpse into the country's architectural history. Many of these houses are open to the public, providing insight into Korean lifestyle and culture.
Seoraksan National Park: Seoraksan is one of South Korea's most iconic national parks, renowned for its breathtaking landscapes and diverse ecosystems. Visitors can hike scenic trails, explore Buddhist temples like Sinheungsa, and enjoy panoramic views from the park's peaks. The vibrant fall foliage attracts tourists during autumn.
Gyeongju: Known as the "museum without walls," Gyeongju is a city with a rich cultural and historical heritage. It was the capital of the ancient Silla Kingdom, and today, it boasts numerous historical sites such as Bulguksa Temple, Seokguram Grotto, and the ancient observatory Cheomseongdae. Gyeongju is also home to beautiful parks and traditional Korean architecture.
