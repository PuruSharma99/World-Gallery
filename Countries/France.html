Eiffel Tower (Tour Eiffel): The Eiffel Tower is one of the most recognizable structures in the world and a symbol of Paris. Visitors can take an elevator or climb the stairs to the top for panoramic views of the city. Location: Paris.
Louvre Museum (Musée du Louvre): The Louvre is the world's largest art museum and a historic monument in Paris. It is home to thousands of works of art, including the Mona Lisa and the Venus de Milo. Location: Paris.
Mont Saint-Michel: Mont Saint-Michel is a medieval abbey perched on a rocky island in Normandy. The abbey and its surrounding bay are designated UNESCO World Heritage sites and are known for their stunning beauty and architecture. Location: Normandy.
Versailles Palace (Château de Versailles): The Palace of Versailles is a lavish royal residence known for its opulent architecture, beautiful gardens, and the Hall of Mirrors. It played a significant role in French history and is a UNESCO World Heritage site. Location: Versailles, near Paris.
Côte d'Azur (French Riviera): The French Riviera is a glamorous stretch of coastline along the Mediterranean Sea. It includes cities like Nice, Cannes, and Saint-Tropez, known for their beautiful beaches, luxury resorts, and vibrant nightlife. Location: Southeastern France.
